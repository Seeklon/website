Recruitment is undergoing a deep transformation. Studies show that **67% of SMB recruiters** spend more than 10 hours per week on CV screening. That's considerable time that could be invested in higher-value tasks: interviews, onboarding, employer brand development.

For years, recruitment software (ATS) relied on keyword filtering. Result? Qualified candidates rejected over wording differences, and recruiters drowning in irrelevant CVs.

## The problem with keyword matching

### A system that penalizes strong profiles

Keyword filtering relies on binary logic: the term is present or absent. This approach creates two types of costly errors:

**False negatives**: a developer writes "React.js" instead of "ReactJS", a salesperson mentions "business development" in different words. These qualified candidates are automatically filtered out.

**False positives**: conversely, candidates who master "keyword stuffing" pass the filters without having the real skills.

> According to a Harvard Business Review study, traditional ATS eliminate up to 75% of qualified applications due to overly rigid filtering criteria.

### The impact on SMBs

For an SMB that recruits occasionally, the consequences are twofold:

1. **Time lost**: having to manually sort hundreds of CVs to compensate for the system's limits
2. **Talent lost**: the best profiles, often more creative in how they present themselves, slip through the net

## Semantic analysis: understanding meaning, not words

### How does semantic AI work?

Unlike keyword matching, semantic artificial intelligence analyzes the **context** and **meaning** of information. It understands that:

- "Managing a team of 5 people" and "Leading a team of 5 colleagues" refer to the same skill
- A background in tech startups demonstrates adaptability soft skills, even without an explicit mention
- A career change can bring a wealth of transferable skills

### The technologies behind this revolution

Modern semantic analysis relies on several technologies:

- **NLP (Natural Language Processing)**: natural language processing to understand the structure and meaning of sentences
- **Word embeddings**: vector representation of words to measure their semantic proximity
- **Machine Learning**: continuous learning to refine result relevance

## Concrete benefits for recruiters

### 1. Measurable time savings

With an automatic scoring system, screening time goes from hours to minutes. The recruiter gets a list of candidates ranked by relevance, with a 0–5 star score and an explanation of each profile's strengths.

### 2. Stronger objectivity

AI doesn't get tired. It analyzes the 100th CV with the same attention as the first. It isn't influenced by unconscious cognitive biases related to photo, name, address, or school.

### 3. Discovery of atypical profiles

Career changers, self-taught professionals, international profiles... These "hidden gems" often ignored by traditional filters become visible thanks to transferable skills analysis.

### 4. Soft skills detection

Beyond technical skills, semantic AI can identify indicators of soft skills: leadership, learning ability, teamwork. These elements, crucial for hiring success, are hard to detect with simple keyword filtering.
